What is Pitching?

Understanding the fundamentals and technique

Pitching is a fundamental skill in softball that requires precision, technique, and practice. It's the art of delivering the ball to the batter with control and strategy.

The Basics

Pitching involves throwing the ball underhand from the pitcher's mound to home plate. The pitcher must maintain proper form and follow specific rules about their delivery motion.

Technique

Good pitching technique includes proper stance, wind-up, release point, and follow-through. Each element works together to create an effective pitch.

Practice & Skill

Becoming a skilled pitcher takes dedication and consistent practice. Learning different pitch types and refining accuracy are key to improvement.

Strategy

Pitching isn't just about throwing the ball—it's about outsmarting batters by varying speed, location, and pitch types to keep them off balance.

Key Elements of Pitching

Stance & Balance

Maintaining proper balance and positioning on the pitcher's mound

Accuracy

Consistently hitting the target and controlling where the ball goes

Power & Speed

Generating velocity while maintaining control of the pitch

Mental Focus

Staying concentrated and confident throughout the game
